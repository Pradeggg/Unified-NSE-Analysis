## 1. Research question (one sentence)
What are the key drivers of growth in India's plastics industry, and how can investors capitalize on this trend?

## 2. Definition
- **Industry term**: Plastics and Packaging Industry in India.
- **Universe rule**: NSE-listed companies involved in manufacturing, processing, or packaging of plastic products.
- **Source**: Aligned with industry body reports such as those from Mordor Intelligence and IBERS.

## 3. Hypotheses
### H1 (Sector)
- **One sentence**: The plastics sector is characterized by robust growth due to increasing demand across various sectors, evidenced by a CAGR of 6.30% for the market size reaching USD 60.11 billion by 2030.
- **How we test it**: Analyze historical data on market size and compare with projected growth rates from industry reports.

### H2 (Stock selection)
- **One sentence**: Stocks within the plastics sector that meet fundamental quality criteria have higher returns compared to the Nifty 500 index over a 6-month horizon.
- **How we test**: Screen for companies meeting specific fundamental scores, momentum indicators, and composite weights; backtest performance against the Nifty 500.

## 4. Scope and limits
- **In scope**: Listed Indian plastics manufacturers and packaging firms.
- **Out of scope**: Non-listed entities, non-plastics manufacturing sectors (e.g., metals).
- **Known limits**: Data availability for listed companies only; point-in-time snapshot as of March 9, 2026.

## 5. Screen criteria (operational)
- **Quality**: Fundamental score > 70.
- **Momentum/RS**: Relative Strength (RS) vs Nifty 500 > 0 over the last 6 months.
- **Composite weights**: Based on market capitalization and liquidity metrics.
- **Shortlist size**: Top 15 companies meeting all criteria.
