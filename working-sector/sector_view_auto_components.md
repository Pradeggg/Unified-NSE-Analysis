# Auto Ancillary / Auto Components – Sector View (India)

**Purpose:** Combined sector narrative and research view using the [Sharescart Auto Ancillary industry list](https://www.sharescart.com/industry/auto-ancillary/) (listed universe) and the strategic outlook from *auto-components.md* (policy, EV, ADAS, clusters). Our analysis universe is a subset of this listed space.

---

## I. Listed universe and market size

**Listed Auto Ancillary companies (India):** As of **6 Mar 2026**, [Sharescart](https://www.sharescart.com/industry/auto-ancillary/) reports **113 listed Auto Ancillary companies** with an aggregate **market capitalization of ₹7,53,276 crore**. The list is sorted by market cap and includes CMP, 52W High/Low, PE, PB, ROCE, ROE, PAT TTM, and Div Yield.

**Top names by market cap (illustrative):** Samvardhana Motherson International, Bosch, UNO Minda, Tube Investments of India, Endurance Technologies, Sona BLW Precision Forgings, ZF Commercial Vehicle Control Systems, Tenneco Clean Air India, Craftsman Automation, CIE Automotive India, Belrise Industries, Sansera Engineering, Gabriel India, Motherson Sumi Wiring, Shriram Pistons & Rings, Minda Corporation, JBM Auto, Lumax Auto Technologies, Banco Products, Varroc Engineering, ASK Automotive, Pricol, and others down to small caps.

**Industry turnover (ACMA):** The broader auto-component *industry* (including unlisted) had a turnover of **₹6.73 lakh crore (USD 80.2 billion)** in FY2024–25, with ~9.6% YoY growth and ~14% CAGR over five years. OEM supply, aftermarket, and exports are the main segments. Our **analysis universe** uses **47 NSE-listed** component names (ex-OEM), aligned with ACMA’s component-only definition; the full listed Auto Ancillary space per Sharescart is 113.

## II. Strategic outlook (2025–2026) – from auto-components.md

**Market performance:** The Indian auto-component industry is in a robust upcycle. H1 FY26 (Apr–Sep 2025) sector growth was **6.8%**, reaching **₹3.56 lakh crore (USD 40.0 billion)**. CRISIL has indicated **7–9% revenue growth for FY26**, driven by 2W and PV demand. Top players: Motherson (FY25 consolidated revenue ~₹1,13,663 Cr, 15% growth; booked business >$88 B); Sona Comstar (24% YoY revenue growth Q2 FY26; Q3 FY26 revenue >₹1,200 Cr); Bosch India (leader in Advanced Automotive Technology, premiumization, and electronic content).

**Policy (Budget 2026 & PLI):** Budget 2026–27 shifted emphasis from demand-side subsidies to **supply-chain strengthening**. Key updates: **Auto PLI** allocation **₹5,939.87 Cr** (nearly triple the previous year); **PM E-Drive** ₹1,500 Cr (FY27) for electric trucks and charging; **customs duty** full exemption on 35 capital goods for EV cell manufacturing and 12 critical minerals; **FAME III** subsumed into PM E-Drive. PLI-Auto has expanded its beneficiary roster (e.g. [Livemint](https://www.livemint.com/industry/pli-auto-scheme-production-linked-incentive-scheme-india-electric-buses-auto-components-ev-manufacturing-localisation-11747890314581.html)).

**EV component localization:** Localization is **30–40%** (early 2026), with a goal to halve battery imports by 2027. “Bharat Cell” (Ola 4680), magnet-free motors (Ola ferrite), and operational gigafactories (e.g. Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Telangana) are scaling. Non-traditional components (batteries, semiconductors) are **40–50%** of EV value. EV component share of OEM shipments reached **~4.6%** by early 2026.

**ADAS & premiumization:** ADAS penetration in new PVs reached **8.41%** in 2025 (JATO); projected **62%** by 2035 (~25% CAGR). ADAS mandated for CVs from April 2026. Automotive sunroof market is projected to **$1.57 B by 2031** (~13.88% CAGR). Thermal management is evolving (liquid/immersion cooling) for high ambient temperatures.

**Regional clusters:** Production is concentrated in **Tamil Nadu** (e.g. Thoothukudi, Hosur/Krishnagiri; VinFast, Uno Minda investments), **Maharashtra** (Pune–Chakan–Talegaon; 750+ firms, 80% of Pune warehouse leasing), **Gujarat** (Sanand; Tata AutoComp, Suzuki EV investments), and **Haryana/NCR** (Manesar, Rohtak; Aisin, component expansions).

**Category mix:** ICE engines & transmission remain stable (5–7% growth, 90–95% localized). EV powertrain is high growth (15%+), localization 30–40%. Power electronics and interiors/body (ADAS, HMI, sunroofs) are rising.

**Strategic challenges:** **~10% cost disability** vs China (logistics/supply chain); export gains (e.g. **$22.9 B**, 8% growth) alongside import surge and marginal trade deficit in some electronics; shift from “push” to “pull” supply chains for inventory and reliability.
