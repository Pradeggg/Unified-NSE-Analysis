## Definition and market size

The automotive components sector in India is defined by the Automotive Component Manufacturers Association of India (ACMA) as parts and systems supplied to vehicle manufacturers (OEMs), **excluding** OEMs (Maruti, Tata Motors, M&M, Bajaj Auto, Hero, Eicher, Ashok Leyland, TVS Motor, etc.). The industry turnover was **₹6.73 lakh crore (USD 80.2 billion)** in FY2024–25, with 9.6% YoY growth and ~14% CAGR over five years. Segments: OEM supply (₹5.70 L Cr), aftermarket (₹99,948 Cr), exports (USD 22.9 B).

The **listed** Auto Ancillary space is broader than turnover alone. As of **6 Mar 2026**, [Sharescart](https://www.sharescart.com/industry/auto-ancillary/) lists **113 listed Auto Ancillary companies** in India with an aggregate **market capitalization of ₹7,53,276 crore**, sorted by market cap (top names include Samvardhana Motherson, Bosch, UNO Minda, Tube Investments, Endurance, Sona BLW, ZF CV, Craftsman, Gabriel, Motherson Sumi Wiring, Shriram Pistons, Minda, JBM Auto, Lumax Auto Technologies, Banco, Varroc, ASK Automotive, Pricol, and others). Our **analysis universe** uses **47 NSE-listed** component names (ex-OEM) for the pipeline and screens; the full listed set is 113.

## Strategic outlook (2025–2026)

**Performance:** The sector is in a robust upcycle. H1 FY26 (Apr–Sep 2025) growth was **6.8%** (₹3.56 L Cr / $40 B). CRISIL projects **7–9% revenue growth in FY26** on 2W and PV demand. Motherson reported FY25 consolidated revenue ~₹1,13,663 Cr (15% growth); Sona Comstar 24% YoY in Q2 FY26; Bosch leads in advanced automotive tech and premiumization.

**Policy:** Budget 2026–27 emphasised supply-chain over demand subsidies. **Auto PLI** allocation **₹5,939 Cr** (up sharply); **PM E-Drive** ₹1,500 Cr (FY27) for e-trucks and charging; customs exemption on EV cell capital goods and critical minerals; FAME III folded into PM E-Drive.

**EV & tech:** EV supply-chain localization **30–40%** (target: halve battery imports by 2027). EV component share of OEM shipments **~4.6%** (early 2026); non-traditional parts (batteries, semiconductors) **40–50%** of EV value. **ADAS** penetration in new PVs **8.41%** (2025), projected **62%** by 2035; ADAS mandated for CVs from Apr 2026. Sunroof and thermal-management segments are high-growth.

**Clusters:** Tamil Nadu (Thoothukudi, Hosur/Krishnagiri), Maharashtra (Pune–Chakan–Talegaon, 750+ firms), Gujarat (Sanand, Tata AutoComp, Suzuki), Haryana/NCR (Manesar, Rohtak) are the main hubs.

**Challenges:** ~10% cost disability vs China (logistics); exports **$22.9 B** (8% growth) but import surge in some electronics; shift to “pull” supply chains for efficiency.

## Why this sector for research

The auto components segment is large, under-researched relative to OEMs, and aligned with PLI, EV, and export themes. Focusing on **listed** ancillary suppliers (our 47-name universe or the full 113 on Sharescart) gives a clear, investable lens on the value chain. *sector_view_auto_components.md* and *auto-components.md* detail sources and narrative; *hypothesis_auto_components.md* and *literature_notes_auto_components.md* set the research question and cross-checks.
