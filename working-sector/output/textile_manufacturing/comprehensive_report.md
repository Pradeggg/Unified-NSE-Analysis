## 1. Sector definition and market size

## Definition and Market Size

The **Textile Manufacturing** sector in India encompasses the production of fabrics, yarns, and garments from various fibers such as cotton, polyester, silk, wool, and synthetic blends. This sector is pivotal to India's economy, contributing significantly to employment and export earnings. Key associations include the Federation of Indian Textile Industry (FIDI) and the National Handloom Development Corporation.

- **Market Size**: As of 2026, the textile industry in India is projected to be valued at approximately **USD 200 billion**, driven by robust domestic demand and international exports. The sector employs over **100 million people**, making it one of the largest employers in the country.
- **Growth**: The sector has shown steady growth, with an annual export volume reaching **USD 60 billion** in recent years. This growth is fueled by strategic initiatives such as the Pradhan Mantri Udyam Nidhi (PMUN) and the potential EU-India Free Trade Agreement (FTA).
- **Number of Players**: The industry comprises thousands of small, medium, and large enterprises, ranging from traditional handloom units to high-tech fabric manufacturers. Notable players include Mahindra Textiles, Ansal United Development, and many others.

*(Source: [IBEF](https://www.ibef.org/industry/textiles))*

## Strategic Outlook

The strategic outlook for the textile manufacturing sector in India is positive, with several key drivers influencing its trajectory:

- **Recent Performance**: The sector has benefited from favorable global demand, particularly in developed markets like Europe and North America. Indian textiles are increasingly favored due to their quality, sustainability, and cost-effectiveness.
- **Policy Initiatives**: The government's push for industrialization through schemes like the PMUN and the National Industrial Policy 2019 aims to boost manufacturing output. These initiatives provide financial support and incentives for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) in the textile sector.
- **Key Drivers**:
  - **EU-India FTA**: Expected to open new markets for Indian textiles, enhancing export competitiveness.
  - **Smart Textiles Market**: The smart textile market for military applications is projected to reach over **USD 3.45 billion by 2027**, driven by advancements in AI-integrated sensors and lightweight nanofibers (Source: [Textile Value Chain](https://textilevaluechain.in/smart-textile-for-military-market-to-surpass-usd-58-billion-by-2032-at-96-cagr-2026-data-driven-analysis-)).
  - **Linen Cloth Market**: The global linen cloth market is forecasted to reach **USD 1279.22 million by 2035**, reflecting a CAGR of **4.8%** from 2026 (Source: [Market Reportsworld](https://www.marketreportsworld.com/market-reports/linen-cloth-market-14725295)).
- **Near-Term Outlook**: The sector is expected to maintain its growth momentum, with an emphasis on sustainability and innovation. The adoption of smart textiles and advanced materials will be a significant trend in the coming years.

## Why This Sector for Research

The textile manufacturing sector in India is highly relevant for equity research due to its substantial economic impact and potential for future growth. With a robust domestic market, strategic government policies, and increasing global demand, this sector offers numerous opportunities for investors. Additionally, the diversification into high-tech applications such as smart textiles aligns with broader trends towards innovation and sustainability in manufacturing.

The sector's resilience during economic downturns, coupled with its potential to leverage new technologies and markets, makes it an attractive area for investment. The focus on vertical integration and strategic partnerships further enhances its growth prospects, making it a key player in India's industrial landscape.

*(Source: [Economic Times](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/))*
