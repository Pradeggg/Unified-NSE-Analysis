## 1. Sector definition and market size

The plastics sector in India encompasses the production of various plastic products, including packaging materials, consumer goods, automotive parts, and construction components. According to multiple sources (Mordor Intelligence, ResearchandMarkets), the market size for the plastic industry in India was expected to reach USD 44.28 billion by 2025 and further surge to USD 60.11 billion by 2030, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.30%. The Indian plastics packaging market is anticipated to expand from USD 22.44 billion in 2025 to USD 26.13 billion by 2030, reflecting a CAGR of 3.09%.

(General knowledge: The plastic industry's value was also forecasted to rise to USD 79.99 billion by 2034 with an 8.49% CAGR.)

## Strategic Outlook

Recent performance and policy initiatives have significantly bolstered the sector's growth. Export targets are set for the plastics sector to reach US$ 25 billion by 2027, a doubling from about $10 billion (NewKerala). Key drivers include infrastructure development, increased consumer demand across sectors like construction, automotive, and consumer goods, and government policies aimed at promoting sanitation and hygiene practices (IBEF).

(General knowledge: The plastics industry in India is a major contributor to the economy, driven by its growth in various sectors.)

## Why This Sector for Research

The plastics sector in India is highly relevant for equity research due to its substantial market size, robust growth projections, and significant contribution to economic development. It is also an under-researched area with ample opportunities for investment, aligning well with themes of infrastructure growth and sustainable consumption patterns. The sector's resilience and adaptability to global trends make it a critical focus for investors seeking exposure to high-growth markets in Asia.

(General knowledge: The plastics industry's expansion reflects broader economic development trends in India.)
